## Pain Points

**Money Pains:**
- Etsy fees eating 15-20% of every sale
- Can't justify big investment when profit margins are thin
- Husband skeptical of spending more money on "the hobby"

**Time Pains:**
- After work + dinner + kids' bedtime = maybe 1 hour
- That hour is precious, can't waste it on tech problems
- Learning curve feels like a luxury she doesn't have

**Tech Pains:**
- Not afraid of tech, just doesn't have time to troubleshoot
- Needs things to work the FIRST time
- Can't spend weekends debugging

**Family/Life Pains:**
- Feels guilty taking time away from kids to work on store
- Spouse doesn't fully "get it" - sees it as hobby not business
- Exhausted, running on fumes

## Relatable Stories

**Story 1 - The 11pm Setup Attempt**:
Sarah finally had a free evening. Kids asleep by 8pm. She started a Shopify trial at 8:30. By 11pm, she'd picked a theme but couldn't figure out shipping settings. She had work at 7am. She closed the laptop and didn't open it again for a month.

**Story 2 - The Etsy Fee Gut Punch**:
Her best month ever - $2,000 in sales! Then she saw the fees: transaction fees, payment processing, listing fees, ads fees. She kept $1,400. She spent 40+ hours making those products. That's $35/hour before materials. Her day job pays better.

**Story 3 - The Customer Request**:
A customer DM'd: "Do you have a website? I want to buy more but Etsy is annoying." Sarah said "coming soon!" That was 8 months ago. She's sent that same message to 12 customers. She knows she's leaving money on the table.
