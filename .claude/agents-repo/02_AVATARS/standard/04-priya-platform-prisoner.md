## Pain Points

**Platform Risk Pains:**
- Account can be suspended for things outside her control
- Competitors file fake IP claims
- Policy changes happen overnight
- Algorithm changes tank rankings instantly

**Ownership Pains:**
- Doesn't own her customer list (Amazon does)
- Can't email her own customers
- Reviews live on Amazon, not her brand
- If banned, starts from zero

**Money Pains:**
- Amazon takes 30-40% (FBA fees, referral fees, PPC)
- PPC costs rising every year
- Race to the bottom on pricing
- Competitors can copy listings

**Brand Pains:**
- Customers think they bought from "Amazon" not her brand
- No relationship building
- Can't control the experience
- Zero brand equity being built

## The Wake-Up Calls She's Had

1. **The Suspension Scare**: Account got suspended for 3 days over a baseless IP claim. She lost $8K in sales and aged 5 years in stress. Got reinstated but realized it could have been permanent.

2. **The Competitor Clone**: A Chinese seller copied her listing, undercut her by 30%, and ran fake reviews. Amazon did nothing for months. She lost her #1 ranking.

3. **The Fee Increase**: Amazon raised FBA fees again. Her margins went from 35% to 22% overnight. She had no choice but to accept.

---

## Relatable Stories

**Story 1 - The 3am Suspension Email**:
Priya woke up to an email: "Your selling privileges have been removed." Her heart stopped. A competitor filed a fake trademark claim. It took 17 days and a lawyer ($2K) to get reinstated. 17 days of $0 revenue. She swore she'd never be 100% dependent on Amazon again.

**Story 2 - The Customer She Couldn't Keep**:
A customer DM'd her on Instagram: "I love your products, I've bought 5 times!" Priya couldn't find them in any database. They were HER customer, but she had zero data on them. No email. No purchase history. Amazon owned that relationship.

**Story 3 - The Brand Equity Realization**:
Priya met a DTC founder at a conference. Same revenue, but he had 50,000 email subscribers, 30% repeat customers, and offers from investors. She had... revenue. That's it. No list. No brand equity. No asset. Just cash flow that could disappear.
