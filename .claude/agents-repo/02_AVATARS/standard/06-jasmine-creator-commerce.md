## Pain Points

**Monetization Pains:**
- Has followers but income is inconsistent (brand deal feast/famine)
- Print-on-demand margins are garbage (makes $3-5/shirt)
- Audience asks "where can I buy?" and she doesn't have good answer
- Link in bio tools are limited

**Platform Pains:**
- Algorithm controls her income
- Platform could ban her tomorrow
- Doesn't own her audience (they're Instagram's audience)
- Can't email followers directly

**Time Pains:**
- Content creation is already full-time
- Can't spend weeks learning ecommerce
- Needs something that runs itself once set up
- Every hour on store setup is an hour not creating

**Credibility Pains:**
- Wants to be taken seriously as a brand
- Print-on-demand feels cheap
- Doesn't want janky checkout experience
- Wants professional, not "small influencer with merch"

## Relatable Stories

**Story 1 - The DM That Hurt**:
A follower DM'd: "I wanted to buy your hoodie but the checkout was weird and I gave up." Jasmine apologized. She knew her Printful integration was janky. She lost a sale because her "store" wasn't really a store.

**Story 2 - The Brand Deal Drought**:
Three months with zero brand deals. Jasmine's income: $400 (affiliate links). Meanwhile, her friend with 30K followers had a Shopify store doing $5K/month. The friend said "your audience is bigger than mine, why don't you have a store?" Jasmine didn't have a good answer.

**Story 3 - The "Where Can I Buy" Comments**:
Every product video gets comments: "Where can I buy this?" "Link?" "Do you sell this?" Jasmine pins her Linktree. Half the people don't click through. The other half land on a page with 15 links and get confused. She's watched money walk away for a year.
