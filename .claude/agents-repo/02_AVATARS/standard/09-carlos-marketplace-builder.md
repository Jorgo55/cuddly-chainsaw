## Pain Points

**Cost Pains:**
- Marketplace dev quotes: $50K-150K
- Enterprise SaaS: $500-2000/month
- Can't justify that spend before validating
- Catch-22: need platform to validate, need validation to justify platform

**Complexity Pains:**
- Multi-vendor is technically complex
- Needs: seller onboarding, commissions, payouts, disputes
- Can't build this himself
- Even "no-code" solutions don't handle multi-vendor well

**Time Pains:**
- Custom dev timeline: 6-12 months
- Wants to launch MVP and iterate
- Competition might beat him to market
- Every month waiting is opportunity cost

**Validation Pains:**
- How does he test marketplace concept without building it?
- Chicken and egg: need sellers and buyers
- Needs real platform to do real tests
- Can't fake a marketplace with landing pages

## Relatable Stories

**Story 1 - The Dev Quote Shock**:
Carlos pitched his marketplace idea to 5 dev agencies. Quotes came back: $65K, $80K, $95K, $120K, and "depends but at least $50K." He'd saved $30K for this. He was $35K-90K short. He started looking at ways to raise money before building. Then he found another option.

**Story 2 - The Sharetribe Trap**:
Carlos tried Sharetribe. $99/month seemed reasonable. Then he hit limitations. Couldn't customize checkout. Couldn't do the commission structure he wanted. Couldn't export his data easily. He was building on rented land again. He cancelled after 2 months.

**Story 3 - The Competitor Launch**:
A competitor launched a similar marketplace concept. Basic, but live. They were getting press, getting sellers, building momentum. Carlos was still "researching the best technical approach." He realized he was engineering himself into paralysis while others were shipping.
