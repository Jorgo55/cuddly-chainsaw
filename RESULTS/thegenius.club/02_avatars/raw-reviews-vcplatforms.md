## SUMMARY: KEY LANGUAGE PATTERNS FOR COPY MINING

### Top Emotional States (frequency order):
1. **Frustration / anger** — at the process, at specific investors, at the system
2. **Exhaustion / drain** — emotional, physical, mental depletion
3. **Confusion** — positive meetings leading to rejection, contradictory signals
4. **Self-doubt** — questioning idea, career, identity after repeated rejection
5. **Betrayal / broken promises** — verbal yeses that become nos, checks that never arrive
6. **Isolation / loneliness** — nobody understands, carrying the burden alone
7. **Injustice / rigged system** — network-based, class-based, gender-based barriers
8. **Desperation** — running out of runway, options narrowing
9. **Imposter syndrome** — building with every meeting
10. **Defiance** — "I'm done with this" turning point

### Top Phrases / Language Hooks:
- "soft no" / "let's keep in touch" / "not the right fit"
- "ghosted" / "radio silence" / "deafening silence"
- "brutal" / "horrific" / "soul crushing" / "killing me"
- "95% rejection rate" / "100+ nos" / "200 meetings"
- "the buzz didn't translate into investments"
- "the capital never arrived"
- "done thinking investors were the smartest people in the room"
- "less meritocratic" / "medieval" / "broken system"
- "emotional manipulation" / "gaslighting" / "wealth gatekeeping"
- "well-liked entrepreneurs with shitty ideas get funded"
- "I almost stopped fundraising because I was so down"
- "questioning yourself, your idea, your sanity"

### Avatar-Relevant Buckets:

**For the Technical Founder / Expert Bottleneck:**
- "great product" doesn't equal fundable
- investors care about business model, not tech
- "product won't win, distribution will"
- spending 90% of pitch on features misses the mark

**For the Bootstrapped / Reluctant Raiser:**
- "the longer you can hold out, the richer you'll be"
- "VC is like strapping a rocket to your back"
- forced to consider raising after hitting a plateau
- fear of losing control and ownership dilution

**For the Second-Time Founder / Pitch-Deck Prisoner:**
- "even founders who raised a Series A didn't feel confident"
- surprise at difficulty second time around
- "I sort of ignored the warning and then this is killing me"
- knows the game but still can't win

**For the Red-Liner:**
- "years of high-pressure work that can be bad for your health"
- "falling into a tunnel that was getting narrower and narrower"
- neglecting self while fundraising
- profitable but can't access growth capital
