# Unique Mechanism: The Business Infrastructure Audit (BIA)

---

## Structure

**Name**: The Business Infrastructure Audit (BIA)

**What It Is**: A diagnostic framework that maps exactly where a founder's business breaks under scale — operationally, psychologically, and financially. Identifies the 3–5 infrastructure gaps that cause investor rejections before the pitch even matters.

**Why It Works**: Investors don't invest in ideas, pitches, or even traction. They invest in machines — businesses with systems that scale independently of the founder. The BIA evaluates a business through the SAME lens investors use: founder dependency, operational repeatability, team depth, financial infrastructure, and scalability architecture. It reveals what investors see but never say in their polite "soft no" emails.

**Why Others Don't Have It**: Every competitor in this space fixes pitches. We fix what's BEHIND the pitch. The BIA combines three layers nobody else touches simultaneously:

1. **Operational Audit** — SOPs, delegation frameworks, team capability, process documentation, system fragility
2. **Founder Psychology Audit** — delegation blocks, control patterns, identity fusion, burnout indicators
3. **Investor-Lens Evaluation** — scoring the business on the exact 14 dimensions VCs evaluate during due diligence

Built from Andy Murphy's 23 years working with 23+ IPO founders — pattern-matched across what actually gets funded vs. what keeps getting "soft nos."

## Named Sub-Components (for value stacking)

| Component | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Founder Dependency Score** | How much of the business dies if the founder disappears for 90 days |
| **Operational Repeatability Index** | % of core processes that are documented and executable by someone other than the founder |

| **Team Depth Assessment** | Whether the org chart has real people or just the founder wearing multiple hats |
